Layer 1 — Generative AI
Generative AI: The Fastest Path to Value
Start here because it uses existing data, existing tools, and existing workflows. No new infrastructure.

Three Deployable Use Cases

Advisor Copilot

Advisors type questions in plain English: "What's driving spend decline at First National in grocery?" AI pulls from Card Expert data and answers with sourced charts. No new data needed — just a GenAI layer on existing APIs.

QBR Draft Generation

AI pre-generates 80% of the quarterly business review: portfolio performance narrative, peer benchmarks, trend callouts. Advisor reviews, personalizes, delivers. Cuts prep from 15-20 hours to 2-3 hours.

Campaign Copy Variants

Given a campaign brief, GenAI produces 5-8 copy variants per channel (email, SMS, push). Marketing team selects, compliance reviews, then deploys. Same approval flow — just faster drafting.

Why This Works at Fiserv

  • No new data pipelines: All three use cases work with data already in Card Expert and existing CRM systems
  • No BAU disruption: Advisors keep their workflow. The copilot is a new tab, not a new process
  • Measurable in 60 days: Time-to-QBR, advisor satisfaction, campaign variant volume — all trackable immediately
  • Low investment: GenAI APIs (Azure OpenAI / Anthropic) + thin integration layer. 2-3 engineers for 8 weeks
  • Builds organizational muscle: Teams learn to work with AI output before we introduce automation
Honest Risk

GenAI can hallucinate portfolio numbers. Mitigation: every AI-generated insight must cite the source data row from Card Expert. If it can't cite, it doesn't show. No unsourced claims.

Layer 2 — Market Mix Model
AI-Enhanced Market Mix Model
Answer the question every FI asks: "Which of our portfolio growth tactics are actually working?"

What MMM Adds to the Advisory

Today we advise FIs on tactics based on experience and benchmarks. MMM gives us quantitative proof of what works — per FI, per channel, per tactic.

1
Channel Attribution

Which channel drove the reactivation — email, push, SMS, or the rewards offer? MMM decomposes the contribution.

2
Diminishing Returns Detection

Shows when a channel is saturated. "You've hit the ceiling on direct mail for this FI — reallocate to digital."

3
Budget Scenario Planning

"If we shift $50K from mail to push, projected incremental spend lift is 4.2%." Advisors bring this to QBRs as data-backed recommendations.

How It Fits the Advisory Model

MMM doesn't automate anything. It makes the advisor's recommendation evidence-based instead of experience-based.

Why This Sequence Matters

MMM needs campaign data to model. Layer 1 (GenAI) increases campaign volume and tagging quality. So Layer 1 literally creates the data that makes Layer 2 work.

Honest Risk

MMM requires 12-18 months of clean, tagged campaign data to be robust. Initial models will be directional, not precise. We should set that expectation with FIs upfront.

Layer 3 — Agentic Workflows
Agentic AI: Automating What We've Already Proven
Not autonomous robots. Automated workflows with human checkpoints — deployed only on proven plays.

What "Agentic" Actually Means Here

Forget the hype. In our context, an "agent" is a workflow automation that chains together detection → analysis → draft action → human approval → execution → measurement.

It's not AI making decisions on its own. It's AI doing the prep work so a human can approve and ship in hours instead of weeks.

Three Starter Workflows

Dormant Card Alert + Draft Campaign

Auto-detects dormant cards above threshold → segments by reactivation likelihood → drafts campaign → queues for advisor review.

Digital Wallet Activation Play

Identifies cardholders not enrolled in Apple/Google Pay → creates targeted push sequence → advisor approves send.

Spend Decline Early Warning

Flags FI portfolios with 2+ weeks of declining spend vs. peers → generates root-cause hypothesis → alerts advisor with talking points.

Why We Wait Until Layer 3

  • You can't automate what you don't understand. Layers 1-2 teach us which plays work, which channels convert, what content resonates. Layer 3 automates the winners.
  • Trust must be earned. Advisors need to see AI-generated content, check it, correct it, and build confidence before we put it in automated loops.
  • Compliance needs precedent. Legal and risk teams need to see 2-3 quarters of AI-generated content going through review before they'll approve any level of automation.
  • FIs need to opt in. Community banks aren't going to accept "AI ran a campaign on your portfolio." They need to be walked into it gradually through the advisory relationship.
Honest Risk

Agentic AI at a regulated finserv company requires regulatory engagement early — not after build. Our legal, compliance, and MRM teams must be in the room from month 1, not brought in at deployment.

Key Difference from v1 Deck

V1 proposed 4 agents running simultaneously from day one. This version says: start with 3 specific workflows, only on proven playbooks, only with human approval gates. Scale what works.

Client Reality
FI Readiness — Not Every Client Is Ready for AI
We segment our FI clients by readiness, not treat them all the same.

FI Readiness Tiers

Tier 1: AI-Ready (10-15% of base)

Large community and regional FIs with dedicated marketing teams, digital maturity, and appetite for innovation. Start here. These FIs will co-pilot with us and provide case studies.

Tier 2: AI-Curious (30-40% of base)

Mid-size FIs interested in better analytics but cautious about automation. Serve with GenAI copilot and reports. No automated campaigns — just better insights for their teams.

Tier 3: Traditional (50%+ of base)

Small FIs with limited marketing resources. Serve with enhanced Card Expert dashboards and pre-built reports. They benefit from AI without knowing it's AI — it just looks like better Fiserv service.

Why Tiering Matters

Pushing agentic workflows on a $400M community bank with no digital marketing team is a recipe for churn, not growth.

  • Tier 1 FIs get the full AI stack — copilot, MMM, agentic workflows. They opt into specific automations and provide feedback.
  • Tier 2 FIs get GenAI-powered insights and reports. The advisor still does the work, just faster. AI is invisible to the FI.
  • Tier 3 FIs get better default reports and benchmarks. The AI improvement is in our production of reports, not in their consumption.
  • Revenue model: Tier 1 = premium advisory upsell. Tier 2-3 = efficiency gain for Fiserv (serve more FIs per advisor).

V1 Flaw

V1 assumed a homogeneous FI base all wanting AI campaigns. Reality: most FIs want better advice, not more automation. We meet them where they are.

Governance
AI Governance: Built-In, Not Bolted-On
Every SVP's first question is "what about risk?" — here's our answer.

Governance Architecture

Key Safeguards

  • Tiered approval: Low-risk actions (report drafts) auto-approved. Campaign sends → advisor + compliance review. New workflow types → SVP sign-off.
  • Full audit trail: Every AI-generated output logged with inputs, model version, and reasoning. Regulator-ready from day one.
  • Hallucination prevention: All AI outputs must cite source data rows. No unsourced claims surface to advisors or FIs.
  • Kill switches: Any AI workflow can be paused instantly at portfolio, FI, or system level. Human override always available.
  • Existing MRM framework: All AI models go through Fiserv's Model Risk Management. No "shadow AI."

Governance Is Phase 1, Not Phase 3

We stand up the governance committee in the first 30 days — before any AI output reaches a client. The guardrails are built before the engines.
